A Random Shakespeare-Style Note
Upon a moonlit road, where quiet shadows danced,
A wandering fool did greet the stars and laugh.
“O gentle night,” quoth he, “lend me thy silver coat,
For I must chase tomorrow before it wakes.”
The trees did bow, the wind replied in whispers,
And somewhere far, a candle fought the dark.
Thus ends this tiny tale, not grand nor wise,
But strange enough to live upon a page.
